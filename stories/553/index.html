Baboon's ss'o |a and hair used as charms against illness

Title

Baboon's ss'o |a and hair used as charms against illness

Collection

Lucy Lloyd |xam notebooks

Summary

...further described the value attached, by the Bushmen, to Ss’ó/õä found in the possession of Baboons; also its great use to the latter. The hair of the Baboon considered as a charm (L V.-24. 5957-5967). Baboons keep Ssho |oa (ss'o |a) in their cheek, which people take out and keep when they kill them. They believe that this stick of Ssho |oa prevents the baboon from feeling pain and illness. Baboons live long and don't know illness, but if they do get ill it is severe and kills them.

Comments

1) Ss'o-|a is also spelt Ssho |oa, 2) p.5956v: Dia!kwain's father (Xaa-ttin) told him that ss'o-|a is kept in the baboon's left cheek, 3) p.5963v: see Chippings made by Dia!kwain's father (before the time of the 'Boers'), 4) p.5964v: Dia!kwain's father (Xaa-ttin) told him most of this story; the rest is from Dia!kwain's personal experience, 5) See also Baboons speak Bushman and have wives, 6) This story is found in Book V-24
