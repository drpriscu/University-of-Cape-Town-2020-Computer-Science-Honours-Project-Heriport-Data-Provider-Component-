The Mantis pretends he has left one of his veldschoens behind and becomes a Lion

Title

The Mantis pretends he has left one of his veldschoens behind and becomes a Lion

Collection

Lucy Lloyd |xam notebooks

Summary

The Ichneumon ... proceeds to comment upon some other doings of the Mantis, who – wanting to roast the little 'Löffelhund' (Proteles) – is himself roasted by its incensed mother; and, escaping, dips his singed wings into the water, and comes forth a renovated Mantis (L II.-9. 997-1002, 10. 1003-1051). |kaggen (the Mantis) becomes a Lion after burning in the fire. The Lion wishes to marry the |ki and falls in the fire. |kaggen hides in a sack and frightens the Lion cub at the Lion's house.

Comments

1) Date on p.999: 15 December; p.1040: 9 January, 2) This is a lengthy story and follows into others concerning the lion and its doings (as |kaggen has become a Lion), 3) See also The Ichneumon rebukes the Mantis for his ill-deeds and |kwammana, accompanied by the Mantis and the young Ichneumon, visits the house of the |ku, 4) This story is found in Books II-9 & II-10
