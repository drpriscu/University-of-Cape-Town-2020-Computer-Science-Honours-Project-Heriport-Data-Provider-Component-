Doings of the Mantis when the eland has been wounded

Title

Doings of the Mantis when the eland has been wounded

Collection

Lucy Lloyd |xam notebooks

Summary

An account of the doings of the Mantis when an Eland has been wounded. The protection afforded by the Mantis to the Eland is also alluded by Díä!kwãin, in 'Rules to be observed when an Eland has been shot, etc.' (L VIII.-23. 8033-8039). When an eland has been wounded, |kaggen (the Mantis) is angry with the people (he 'does not love us if we kill an eland'). He does various things to trouble the man who shot the eland and thus make the eland get up and live and not die from its wounds. Also, when the people shoot gemsbok, hares appear, and if people shoot the hares then the gemsbok will recover and live.
