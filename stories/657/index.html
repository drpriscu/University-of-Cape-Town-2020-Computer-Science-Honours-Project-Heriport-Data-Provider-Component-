The ||kerri: a bird which eats locusts; and locust-hunting

Title

The ||kerri: a bird which eats locusts; and locust-hunting

Collection

Lucy Lloyd |xam notebooks

Summary

The ||kerri bird eats locusts and is thus called the locust-bird. They drive the locusts into the bushes, where the people collect them and roast them in a fire. The people thirst after collecting locusts and need water. The rain then breaks and the people go and hunt for springbok.
