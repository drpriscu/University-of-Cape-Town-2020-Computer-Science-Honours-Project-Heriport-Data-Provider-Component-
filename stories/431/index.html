The owl and the black crow

Title

The owl and the black crow

Collection

Lucy Lloyd |xam notebooks

Summary

The Owl and the Black Crow foretell the approach of the Lion. By Dia!kwãin (L V.-9. 4689-4706 1/2, 4696 rev., and 4697 rev.). Parents teach that when the owl screams and acts a certain way it means a lion is coming to take them in their sleep. The black crow also warns of the coming of the lion.

Comments

1) p.4688v: Dia!kwain heard this story from his mother and father (≠kamme-an and Xa a ttin), 2) p.4701v: see ||kabbo's place, 3) This story is found in Book V-9
