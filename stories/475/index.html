Ssa ka Kumm (the eland's story)

Title

Ssa ka Kumm (the eland's story)

Collection

Lucy Lloyd |xam notebooks

Summary

The hunting of eland and related ceremonies. There are many things the hunter must do to show respect for the eland, as it is a 'magic thing' and |kaggen (the Mantis) will try to revive the game and keep it from dying. The verso notes contain further details of these practices. Pages 5359v-5361v contains warnings about the casting of shadows (translated by Dorothea Bleek).

Comments

1) p.5316v: this story was told to Dia!kwain by his father (Xaa-ttin), 2) Date on p.5338: 26 September, 3) p.5341v: an explanatory note by Dia!kwain (12 December 1875) on the actions of |kaggen (the Mantis) and how he changes his appearance to make people afraid (like at 'Guy Fawkes'), 4) pp.5345v-5348v: see The Mantis and the hunting of eland, 5) p.5353 opp: (4 October 1875) the !num !num moth found in Bushmanland in trees (caught at Mowbray, 3 October), 6) pp.5359v-5361v contains warnings about the casting of shadows (translated by Dorothea Bleek), 7) p.5361v: the throwing of shadows; what occurs after striking the eland with its tail, 8) See also Further ceremonies in cutting up eland, 9) This story is found in Books V-17 and V-18
