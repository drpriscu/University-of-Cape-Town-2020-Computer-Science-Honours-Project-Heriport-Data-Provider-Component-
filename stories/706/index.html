Doings of the springbok and springbok hunting

Title

Doings of the springbok and springbok hunting

Collection

Lucy Lloyd |xam notebooks

Summary

The behaviour of male and female springbok and various hunting strategies.

Comments

1) An insert between pp.7249v & 7250 contains notes on an ancient Japanese practice of divination using bones of an animal (shoulder blade of a deer), from the Westminster Review of July 1878; also includes quotations in German and notes the divination practices of other peoples also using animal bones, 2) p.7240v: (27 July 1878) a |xam reference to the male springbok's 'bitter' kidneys and lack of fear (or 'understanding'), 3) The springbok resemble the Milky Way when a white springbok is in the herd, 4) p.7241v: the name for a white springbok, 5) p.7245v: (28 July 1878) on herds in which white springboks are present, 6) p.7247v: the name of a springbok without young, 7) p.7249v: what different springbok are called, 8) p.7250v: (1 August 1878), 9) p.7251v: the name 'first-heads' for a springbok, 10) p.7257v: see !nanna-sse, 11) p.7258v: (2 August 1878) on the blood clot that lies in the springbok's wound, 12) This story is found in Book VIII-14
