Title

The story of !ko'-g !nuin-tara, or, !ko'-g !nuin-tara and the Day's Heart star

Collection

Lucy Lloyd |xam notebooks

Summary

The Story of !kó–g!nuin–tára, wife of the 'Dawn’s–Heart' Star. By /han≠kass’o (L VIII.–-7. 8393-8432). In a note, in English, after the same narrator, the younger sister of !kó–g!nuin–tára is stated to belong to the Early Race (which preceded the Bushmen in their country), (L VIII.-6. 6546 rev., note). The She-Hyena (who is jealous of !ko'-g !nuin-tara and her child) poisons !ko'-g !nuin-tara and she becomes a Lynx and trots away into the reeds. The She-Hyena adorns herself in !ko'-g !nuin-tara's ornaments and marries the Day's Heart star. The Day's Heart asks why his wife is not attending to the child. The younger sister goes to !ko'-g !nuin-tara so that she may feed her child, but !ko'-g !nuin-tara's memory of who she is becomes increasingly vague, and she advises her sister to no longer come. Meanwhile, the Day's Heart has discovered the deception and goes to his wife in the reeds. She charges out of the reeds, but stops for one of the goats that they have brought with them. The people hold her down and anoint her with goat's blood and she is returned to normal, except for her ears which remain long and pointed so that she can retain her good hearing.
