Title

Told in illustration of the picture of the dance of sorcerers (Mr Stow's number 8 and 9)

Collection

Lucy Lloyd |xam notebooks

Summary

Remarks upon sorcerers, their dress, etc. (in explanation of one of Mr Stow’s copies of Bushmen paintings), by Dia!kwãin (L V.-10. 4744-4750). The dance of sorcerers is called the ||ken. The dancers play the role of the dog and the gemsbok in the dance. The sorcerers watch to see whether the people will laugh at them or show 'understanding' when they are dressed in this way.

Comments

1) Date on p.4750: 9 June, 2) p.4744v: more about the dance of the sorcerers (||ken), 3) p.4745v: about the rings worn in the dance, 3) p.4746v: how the people must behave towards sorcerers, 4) p.4755v: a child's knowledge or education: their parents teach them not to laugh or disrespect others who are 'different', 5) See also Picture of Mr Orpen's, 6) This story is found in Book V-10
