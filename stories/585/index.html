The |khu or Bushman soup-spoon

Title

The |khu or Bushman soup-spoon

Collection

Lucy Lloyd |xam notebooks

Summary

The /khu, or Bushman soup spoon. This is a brush of native manufacture, the stem of which is also used to scratch the fire together. By |han≠kass’o (L VIII.-1. 6083 and 6084). The |khu is made by the men who bind together the back hairs of the |gipp (Proteles, or aardwolf). There are also details about the springbok's 'paxwax', or |kuerri, in this account of the making of the soup spoon.

Comments

1) p.6083v: the springbok's |kuerri, or 'paxwax', is yellow and lies on the bone, 2) see also The !au or shaped rib-bone, used by Bushmen in eating some kinds of food, 3) This story is found in Book VIII-1
