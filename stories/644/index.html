The son of the Wind

Title

The son of the Wind

Collection

Lucy Lloyd |xam notebooks

Summary

The Son of the Wind. By |han≠kass’o (L VIII.-8. 6687-6708). |na ka ti's mother tells him not to say the name of the son of the Wind until his father has made a hut for them. When the father is finished, |na ka ti calls the Wind's name and then runs to the hut. The Wind blows away the people's houses, except for the Wind's hut. The Wind's mother picks up the son of the Wind and it stops blowing. A strong shelter must be made before calling the Wind's name.

Comments

1) A page inserted between the front cover and frontispiece of Book VIII-8 features a number (III) and a list of dates in chronological order, with |han≠kass'o's movements (before coming to Mowbray) noted alongside, 2) p.6687opp: |han≠kass'o heard this from his mother (|xabbi-an), and a note on her parents and their names; about ||kuarri, 3) p.6687v: see The porcupine eats ||kuarri, 4) 6688v: see Locusts or ||kabba- |kha, 5) p.6694v: see The Wind, 6) p.6706v: a word for a dry stream-bed in |xam and Dutch, 7) See also The Wind and Notes to the story of The Wind, 8) This story is found in Book VIII-8
