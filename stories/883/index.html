A review on the parade

Title

A review on the parade

Collection

Lucy Lloyd |xam notebooks

Summary

|han≠kass'o describes a military parade (at the Grand Parade in Cape Town). The soldiers (British) are standing in rows. He describes their manoeuvres ('they stood still, stood in rows') as well as the movements of a dog which eventually left the parade and returned home.

Comments

1) p.8484v: the train which 'does not give time', 2) According to Lewis-Williams (2000): 127, the parade was observed by the contributor on the occasion of the return of Sir Bartle Frere (the then-governor of the Cape Colony) to Cape Town, 3) This story is found in Book VIII-28
