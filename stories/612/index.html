The Mantis and the Ticks

Title

The Mantis and the Ticks

Collection

Lucy Lloyd |xam notebooks

Summary

The Mantis visits the abode of the Ticks, attempts to take some of their food, and is well beaten by them. He flies away, goes into the water, and returns home where he is pitied and lectured by the Ichneumon. By /han≠kass’o (L VIII.-3. 6236-6258, 6267-6269). Also (L VIII.-20. 7790-7811; L VIII.-20. 7812-7816, 22. 7906-7956).

Comments

1) Date on p.6236 (translation by Dorothea Bleek in October 1919); p.6239: 11 February 1878; p.6258: 16 February, 2) See also The Mantis takes away the Tick's sheep (including Porcupine's speech concerning the coming of ||khwai-hemm) and The Mantis and |kwammana visit the Dassie's house, 3) This story is found in Book VIII-3
