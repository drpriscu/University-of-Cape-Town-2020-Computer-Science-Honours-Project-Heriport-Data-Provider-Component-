!yoa-ka-ttu, the Blue Crane and the girls

Title

!yoa-ka-ttu, the Blue Crane and the girls

Collection

Lucy Lloyd |xam notebooks

Summary

!yoa-ka-ttu, the Blue Crane, and the Girls of the early race. By /han≠kass’o (L VIII.-29. 8526-8554). A creature called !yoa-ka-ttu (or Bad-smelling-mouth, a person of the Early Race) lies in the branches of the !khi tree. The Blue Crane and some girls (of the Early Race) sit in the shade of the same tree and !yoa-ka-ttu breaks wind. They leave, and !yoa-ka-ttu runs after the girls with his assegai. The Blue Crane protects them and is killed and rises up many times. Eventually !yoa-ka-ttu returns to his !khi tree and the Blue Crane returns home.

Comments

1) Date on p. 8534: 21 June, 2) 8525v: |han≠kass'o heard this from his mother (|xabbi-an); !haken resembles 'Bushman rice'; a note about the !khi tree, 3) p.8526v: the Blue Crane is a person of the Early Race and is the elder sister of |kaggen, 4) p.8528v: see Difference between |xam and European methods of articulation, 5) p.8544v: |xam expressions for cutting off meat, 6) This story is found in Book VIII-29
