Cuts to be made on a bow when a baboon has been killed

Title

Cuts to be made on a bow when a baboon has been killed

Collection

Lucy Lloyd |xam notebooks

Summary

Certain cuts to be made upon the bow, when a Baboon has been killed. A similar ceremony to be observed with regard to the Hyena. A Baboon, when killed, resembles a man, in 'making cloud'. In the Katkop dialect, by Díä!kwãin (L V.-24. 5911-5916, 5911 rev., note). Dia!kwain's father told him that the baboon's bad influence, brought about by its death, would live on in (or curse) the bow if the bow was not cut, causing the influence to leave the bow.

Comments
