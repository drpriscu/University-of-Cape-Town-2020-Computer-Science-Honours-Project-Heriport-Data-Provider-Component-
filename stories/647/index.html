Windmaking and springbok hunting

Title

Windmaking and springbok hunting

Collection

Lucy Lloyd |xam notebooks

Summary

Wind–making; followed by a long discourse upon springbok hunting. By /han≠kass’o (L VIII.-8. 6725-6769 rev.). The people beat the bushes with a stick and call to the north wind to help them travel quickly in front of the game. The hunters do not wish to shoot while the springbok are running, as they are too numerous. Some hunters drive, some run and some wait in a screen to shoot the springbok. Bewitched springbok do not fall even when shot, and must be left alone.
