The Moon, not to be looked at, when game is shot

Title

The Moon, not to be looked at, when game is shot

Collection

Lucy Lloyd |xam notebooks

Summary

The Moon is not to be looked at, when game has been shot, for fear of evil consequences. In the Katkop dialect, by Díä!kwãin (LV.-21. 5643-5654). Hunters lower their heads and do not look up when they have shot game, because the Moon's 'water' would cause the game to live (the Moon's water is what the people observe lying on bushes, which resembles 'liquid honey').

Comments

1) p.5642v: Dia!kwain heard this from his mother (≠kamme-an), 2) See also Sneezing: to be avoided when game is shot and Destroying the sneeze or kkoroken, 3) This story is found in Book V-21
