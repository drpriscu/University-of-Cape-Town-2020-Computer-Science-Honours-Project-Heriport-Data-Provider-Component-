Title

How the brother of the maiden taken up in a whirlwind became the Porcupine; followed by various remarks about porcupines

Collection

Lucy Lloyd |xam notebooks

Summary

The Young man (of the early race) who was changed into a porcupine. He is said to be the brother of the girl in the preceding legend. Remarks regarding the porcupine follow. In the Katkop dialect, by Díä!kwãin (L V.-13. 5023-5039, and 14. 5040-5054). A tale of the Early Race (or !khwe ||na ss'e !k'e) in which the brother of the maiden taken up in a whirlwind by the angry Rain is transformed into a Porcupine. Parents tell their children to avoid eating the back of the porcupine's neck and tail, because that is where the hail sits. Also about porcupines and the stars.

Comments

1) p.5022v: this story was told to Dia!kwain by his mother (≠kamme-an), 2) p.5043v: what people call the place where they see the sun ('ostrich eye' or Ttoi tsa xau), 3) See also The maiden who was taken up in a whirlwind by the agency of the angry Rain and became a great snake, 4) This story is found in Books V-13 and V-14
