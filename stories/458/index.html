!nuin-|kui-ten (who was a sorcerer or magician)

Title

!nuin-|kui-ten (who was a sorcerer or magician)

Collection

Lucy Lloyd |xam notebooks

Summary

The sorcerer !nuin|kúïten. In the Katkop dialect, by Díä!kwãin (L V.-15. 5079-5101, 5104-5109). The sorcerer !nuin-|kui-ten becomes a lion and goes about just to look at people, not to hunt them. !nuin-|kui-ten teaches Dia!kwain's father his magic because he feels that he is about to die.

Comments

1) p.5079 opp: this story was told to Dia!kwain by his father (Xaa-ttin) who was taught about '!khwa ka tiken-tiken' by !nuin-|kui-ten when a young man; !nuin-|kui-ten was a friend of Dia!kwain's paternal grandfather, 2) p.5083v: the |xam names for 'patient' and 'impatient' men, 3) See also The broken string and What Xaa-ttin used to sing (the broken string), 4) This story is found in Book V-15
