Xaa-ttin's accident

Title

Xaa-ttin's accident

Collection

Lucy Lloyd |xam notebooks

Summary

Explanation of various personal names was given, in the Katkop dialect, by Díä!kwãin (L V.-10. 4751-4754, 4758-4777, 18. 5373 rev., 19. 5505 rev. and 5506 rev., 20. 5572 and 5573, 5605-5607, translated). About Dia!kwain's father Xaa-ttin who received the name Clay-hand after he had an accident while running after springbok; he got his foot stuck in clay and fell, dislocating his thumb.

Comments

1) p.5571v: Xaa-ttin was Dia!kwain's father's 'great name': the nickname Clay-hand in this story was given to him by his friends; Dia!kwain has forgotten his father's 'little' name, 2) This story is found in Book V-20
