|xue and his wives

Title

|xue and his wives

Collection

Lucy Lloyd !kun notebooks

Summary

This is a story about |xue and his wives (!nanni says that he saw |xue, his house and child while !nanni was with the Herero). |xue resembles a lizard one day, a |ou (a small buck, probably a steenbok) the next, and a little tsema and a ||ha and a python (â‰ ni), all on different days. When |xue dies he resembles the |ou (and has horns). As |xue lies there dead his wives see him and come and pick him up and carry him to the house. The elder (or 'greater') wife makes a fire to cook the kid, and puts his body on the fire. |xue calls out, objecting to being put on the fire. The second wife takes |xue to her house and covers him with a kaross. He bites it and becomes a kid again. The second wife kills him and puts him in a cooking pot, shutting him in and covering him. He becomes an ant and bites his second wife. His wife crushes his ant's head and throws |xue down on the ground.

Comments

1) p.9298v: !nanni heard this from his paternal grandfather (Karu); |xue was a !kun, 2) p.9303v: a race of people called the |ka kue who are like the |nani; they kill the Makoba as well as !kun; they use bows but not guns and come from the same country as the Shimbari and the |nani, 3) p.9306v: the !kun name for 'thinking-strings', 4) This story is found in Book XI & XII-4
