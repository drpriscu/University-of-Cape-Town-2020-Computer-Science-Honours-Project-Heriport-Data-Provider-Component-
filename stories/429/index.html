What the springbok and the gemsbok did when they knew that Dia!kwain's wife would die

Title

What the springbok and the gemsbok did when they knew that Dia!kwain's wife would die

Collection

Lucy Lloyd |xam notebooks

Summary

Death of the narrator’s first wife, which was foretold by springbok and gemsbok. By Dia!kwãin (L V.-9. 4653-4688). An account of what the springbok did when Dia!kwain's first wife (Mietje) died. Ssu-!kui-ten-tta points out that he had told Dia!kwain that the actions of the game foretold what was happening at home.

Comments

1) p.4652v: Dia!kwain's first wife was called Mietje; the names of her parents and where they lived, 2) p.4669v: about Dia!kwain's maat or friend Snore-White-Lying or 'Witbooy', who was taken to the Breakwater before Dia!kwain and died there, 3) p.4671v: the names of Snore-White-Lying's child and another old woman who brought up Dia!kwain's child after his wife died, 4) p.4673v: the |xam name for the hollow at the base of the throat, 5) p.4675v: the name of Dia!kwain's eldest daughter; Snore-White-Lying and his family all lived at their place, 6) p.4678v: Dia!kwain doesn't know how to feed the baby, so asks Snore-White-Lying for advice, 7) p.4683v: the age of Dia!kwain's child when his wife died; about Dia!kwain's niece and the burial of his wife, 8) See also The explanation of the name Ssu-!kui-ten-tta (or Snore-White-Lying), 9) This story is found in Book V-9
