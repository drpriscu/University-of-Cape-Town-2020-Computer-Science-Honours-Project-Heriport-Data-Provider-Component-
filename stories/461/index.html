The dream which Dia!kwain had before he received the tidings of the death of his father

Title

The dream which Dia!kwain had before he received the tidings of the death of his father

Collection

Lucy Lloyd |xam notebooks

Summary

The dream of Díä!kwãin, before he received news of his father’s death. Further omens, etc. In the Katkop dialect by Díä!kwãin (L V.-15. 5110-5146). Dia!kwain dreams that he and his father are cutting up a sheep and a Dutch man ('Boer') threatens to beat them to death for killing the sheep. Dia!kwain awakes at daybreak and tells his wife that the dream had spoken like a person, of things that would come true. Dia!kwain's mother comes to tell him his father has died.

Comments
