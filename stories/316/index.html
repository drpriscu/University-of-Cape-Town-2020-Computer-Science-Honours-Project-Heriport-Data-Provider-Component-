An ignorant man digs up Ssho |oa and the consequences of his actions

Title

An ignorant man digs up Ssho |oa and the consequences of his actions

Collection

Lucy Lloyd |xam notebooks

Summary

An ignorant man having gone to dig up Shó-!õä, is discovered speechless and motionless, sitting among serpents, by the hole where he had been digging. By a skilful application of Shó-!õä, the snakes are driven away, taking with them the scent which had injured the man, but leaving the other scent with the plant in the hole. The man is (also by Shó-|õä) restored to speech and motion (L. II.-36. 3287-3332). The scent of the Ssho |oa plant is then taken away by snakes (which go into the hole that the Ssho |oa was dug out of). The notes accompanying this story describe other things relating to Ssho |oa, such as the Ssho |oa snake. The people never kill Ssho |oa snakes. ||kabbo was told not to dig for Ssho |oa because he didn't have the neccessary marks.
